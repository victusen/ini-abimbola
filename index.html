Biography

About Ini Abimbola

I bring over 25 years of leadership across public and private sectors, advising on institutional and development innovation, strategy development and deployment, public policy execution, stakeholder engagement, external/public relations, sustainability, ESG, and corporate/board governance—often in complex environments where delivery depends on disciplined governance and credible stakeholder alignment.

With 15+ years in development sector program management, I specialize in innovation, Monitoring & Evaluation (M&E), and Results-Based Monitoring & Evaluation (RBME). My expertise lies in helping governments, public agencies, international development agencies, not-for-profits, and corporate boards fix governance and execution breakdowns such as unclear decision rights, weak accountability, stakeholder gridlock, and failed strategies.

As a 2008 Stanford Draper Hills Fellow and Founder/CEO of Thistle Group (consulting and gas interests), I serve on multiple boards including the Mirabel Centre, bringing deep expertise in Nigerian and state government reforms, election oversight (having managed district elections for fair outcomes), and global development initiatives.

I recently earned my DBA in Strategic Management from Rome Business School (2025), further deepening my capacity to architect transformative solutions at the intersection of governance, strategy, and institutional performance.

Public Policy

My Views on Public Service: Enhancing Democratic Processes

Democratic governance thrives on clear accountability, transparent decision-making, and responsive institutions. Drawing from decades of experience managing elections and advising governments, I believe the key to strengthening democratic processes lies in building systems that clarify decision rights, establish credible performance metrics, and foster genuine stakeholder engagement. When citizens trust that their voices matter and see tangible results from governance, democracy flourishes.
